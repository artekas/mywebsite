2Market: Retail Expansion

LSE Project

SQL Tableau Excel View Code on GitHub View Dashboard on Tableau

Project Context

2Market, a global supermarket operating both online and in-store, seeks to refine its marketing and sales strategies. The core challenge lies in unlocking insights from customer demographics, purchasing behaviours, and advertising efficacy.

Objectives

Key questions include:

  1. How can customer segments be better targeted to maximise sales and conversions?
  2. Which advertising platforms have the greatest impact on engagement with key demographic groups?
  3. What product preferences and trends emerge across different customer groups, and how can they inform future marketing strategies?

Analytical Approach

Data Preparation & Cleaning

For this particular project I chose to clean data in Excel

Process Highlights
Ad Data Cleaning
  • Formatted columns and removed decimals
  • Verified and corrected header data types
  • Validated ranges using formulas
  • Checked and removed duplicates
Marketing Data Cleaning
  • Checked for blanks and inconsistencies
  • Formatted data and identified outliers
  • Replaced extreme outlier values (e.g. income 666666) with median
  • Removed duplicate rows excluding unique ID
  • Deleted unmatched IDs across both datasets
  • Formatted dates to SQL-compatible format

SQL-Based Analysis

SQL was used to structure the cleaned data, develop targeted queries, and extract actionable insights across segments.

Process Highlights
Data Import Loaded Excel-cleaned datasets into SQL tables for analysis-ready structure
Query Development Created SQL queries using joins, aggregates, and case statements to group and filter data by country, marital status, and ad campaigns
Customer Profiling Calculated average age and income across key customer segments (e.g. marital status, income tier)
Performance Segmentation Ranked most popular products by demographic and country to inform targeting
Campaign Effectiveness Measured ad effectiveness by comparing purchase behaviour across platforms and segments

Tableau Visualisation & Insights

Tableau was used to explore customer and product trends, visualise web vs in-store preferences, and highlight high-value segments.

Process Highlights
Segment Creation Defined calculated fields for age group and family status (e.g. “Young Adults”, “Child-Free”) to enable clearer comparisons
Channel Split Visualisation Created dual-axis bar + line charts to compare in-store vs web purchases across age groups
Product Spend Analysis Ranked top product categories by total revenue; identified Beverages and Meat as dominant revenue drivers
Household Comparison Compared spending levels by household type, highlighting disproportionate revenue contribution from child-free customers
Marketing Impact Grouped campaigns by channel to compare deal conversion; revealed social media’s dominance in campaign performance

Key Insights

Regional Revenue Contribution

  • Spain leads overall revenue with $634K, followed by South Africa and Canada
  • Montenegro, despite low volume, yields the highest revenue per customer
  • Germany and USA show high efficiency per customer despite smaller overall share

Chart: Spain and South Africa contribute the highest revenue, but Montenegro generates the most revenue per customer.

Purchase Activity by Age Group

  • Established Professionals (45–54) showed the highest purchase activity
  • Closely followed by those Nearing Retirement (55–64) and Retirees (65–74)

Chart: The 45–54 age group drove the highest number of purchases, with strong activity also seen in the 55–64 and 65–74 segments.

Household Type Spending Patterns

  • Child-free customers drove 2–4× higher spending, particularly in alcohol and meat categories
  • They consistently outspent households with children across all product groups

Chart: Child-free customers contribute disproportionately to revenue compared to households with children.

Marketing Effectiveness

Initial assumptions pointed to bulkmail and brochure as strong marketing channels. However, deeper analysis showed a different reality. Social media platforms dominated in deal conversions, both individually and collectively.

Social Platforms Drove 90% of Deals

When grouped together, Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter campaigns generated over 90% of total deals. Traditional channels, by contrast, delivered only a fraction of that performance.

Chart: Social platforms converted 9x more deals than traditional methods.

Bulkmail Leads Individually, but Social Wins Collectively

Among individual channels, Bulkmail generated the highest deal volume. Yet the combined impact of social media channels far outweighed it. Instagram and Twitter followed closely behind, leaving brochures as the lowest performer.

Chart: Bulkmail is strong on its own, but social platforms dominate overall.

Instagram Drove Alcohol Campaign Conversions

Instagram stood out for its performance in alcohol-related campaigns. Though overall deal conversion rates remained below 1%, it consistently delivered stronger engagement in that category.

Chart: Instagram led alcohol deal conversions with relatively higher success rates.

Campaign Performance by Age Group

Instagram and Facebook consistently engaged a broad mix of customer segments.

Chart: Instagram and Facebook consistently performed well across multiple customer types.

Conclusion: While Bulkmail stood out individually, the data clearly supports a shift toward digital-first marketing. Social media platforms, led by Instagram and Facebook, drove stronger performance across all key metrics.

Product & Purchase Trends

  • In-store purchases still dominate across all age groups, especially among older, high-spending shoppers
  • Web shopping is on the rise, but it’s not yet the preferred channel for higher-value purchases
  • Most revenue is driven by just two product categories: Beverages and Meat

Insight: Despite digital growth, traditional in-store shopping remains key to revenue, especially among more affluent, older customers.

Chart: In-store dominates high-spender purchases, while web shopping is steadily growing. Beverages and Meat account for most of the revenue.

Recommendations for 2Market

1. Prioritise In-Store Experience for High-Spending Demographics

In-store purchases dominate among older age groups (45–74), who are also the highest spenders.
  • Invest in targeted in-store promotions (e.g. loyalty cards, senior discount days).
  • Train staff to upsell during peak shopping hours.

2. Strengthen Digital Shopping Experience for Younger Segments

Web purchases are steadily growing among younger customers (18–34), though they spend less per transaction.
  • Improve mobile UX and checkout simplicity.
  • Introduce app-exclusive deals or snack/meat bundles.
  • Retarget cart abandoners with social media ads.

3. Double Down on High-Margin Categories: Beverages & Meat

These two categories drive most of the revenue across both web and in-store channels.
  • Feature them prominently in promotions.
  • Bundle products (e.g. wine & steak).
  • Upsell premium lines to higher-income customers.

4. Segment Campaigns by Household Type

Child-free customers spend 2–4x more, especially on alcohol and meat.
  • Use Instagram and Facebook to target this group with lifestyle-based ads.
  • Launch geo-segmented campaigns in urban zones.

5. Adopt Channel-Specific Campaign Strategy

Social platforms collectively outperform all others, converting over 90% of deals.
  • Prioritise digital ad budgets on Instagram and Facebook.
  • Use Bulkmail for follow-ups with personalised coupons.
  • Phase out underperforming traditional channels like brochures.

6. Tailor Product Promotions by Age Group

Older customers prefer in-store and high-value products; younger ones lean towards convenience and variety.
  • Promote large-pack meat/beverage deals to older customers.
  • Offer sample boxes online for younger users.

7. Monitor and Revisit Spending Tier Definitions

Purchasing behaviour varies significantly by age, channel, and household type.
  • Define Spending Tiers using transaction value + frequency.
  • Use these tiers for tailored marketing, such as exclusive early access offers.
