Project Context

Following a national ice shortage during Spain's 2022 heatwave, Gaea AI partnered with a major ice supplier and supermarket chain to improve supply chain resilience. Our mission was to forecast demand more accurately and avoid future stockouts. As requested by the stakeholder, we focused on petrol stations within 65 km of central Madrid, integrating climate and behavioural patterns to identify demand spikes.

Objectives

  1. Pinpoint spatial and temporal demand drivers for ice sales.
  2. Forecast high-risk weeks for stockouts based on climate and behavioural signals.
  3. Quantify lost revenue from past shortages and optimise future profits.

Analytical Approach

Data Preparation & Cleaning

All datasets were initially cleaned in Excel

Process Highlights
Data Cleaning
  • Removing duplicates and fixing inconsistent city and community names.
  • Mapping station postcodes to distances from Madrid's Plaza Mayor using FreeMapTools.
  • Classifying stations by direction (N, S, E, W) via Google Maps for regional insights.
  • Validating weather data against official meteorological sources, filling gaps with verified records.
  • Integrating manually cross-checked public holidays and festivals (2021–2023).

Toolset

  • Python: predictive modelling
  • Tableau: interactive dashboards, maps, demand visualisation

Exploratory Analysis & Key Insights

Insight: Demand surges above 35°C were often followed by sharp dips, likely due to stockouts — a key operational blind spot.
  • Peak sales consistently aligned with weekends, especially Saturdays during heatwaves.

    • Chart: Weekend peaks in sales, with Saturdays during heatwaves driving the highest volume.

  • Sales were highest in July, followed by June and August. Week-level spikes often matched heatwave periods.

    • Chart: Sales volumes spiked in July, followed by June and August, mirroring seasonal temperature highs.

  • 2022 weekly average temperature was 1.5°C higher than 2021 (16.8°C vs 15.3°C), correlating with surging demand.

    • Chart: 2022 was 1.5°C warmer than 2021 on average — a key factor behind rising demand.

  • Northwest Madrid emerged as the most ice-hungry zone, with the highest-performing stations.

Chart: Sales in 2021 revealed a consistent pattern - the Northwest region was already performing strongly.

Chart: In 2022, Northwest stations once again dominated sales during heatwaves.

Predictive Modelling

  • Built a Multiple Linear Regression model using 2022 weekly data to reduce noise and align with delivery cycles.

    • Chart: Predicted vs actual sales — the model captured seasonality and heat-driven surges with high accuracy.

  • Key predictors: average max temp, station direction, weekend heat flags, public holidays/festivals, and 2-week sales lag.

    • Chart: Hot weekends, public holidays and Northwest were the strongest predictors of demand.

  • Model explained 70% of variation in sales. Residuals increased for high-volume stations, suggesting unmet demand.

Stockout analysis identified stations with actuals >30% below predicted during hot weeks (>28°C). Estimated 2022 lost revenue: €17,612.44, with top 3 stations in NW Madrid.

2023 Forecasting

We extended the model using forecasted 2023 temperatures and official holiday calendars. Weekly demand was predicted iteratively using prior lagged actuals/predictions.

Product Optimisation

Two models were built:

  • Baseline: Demand capped at 28°C to estimate standard cube ice volume.
  • Premium Model: Demand above baseline assumed premium format (higher margin).

Total projected 2023 revenue: €208,751.90, including €50,730.20 from premium ice. Estimated profit uplift: €8,052.40.

Chart: 2023 Product Optimisation Model Leads to €43,000 in Premium Ice Revenue

Recommendations for GAEA_AI

  • Trigger-Based Restocking. Restock when max daily temperature exceeds 28°C and inventory drops below 30%.
  • Focus on Top 25 Stations by Lost Revenue. Concentrate supply efforts on high-loss stations, mostly in the NW region.
  • Apply Regional Product Mix. Shift premium formats to heat-prone, high-demand regions during surges to boost margin.
  • Weekly Operational Integration. based on rolling averages and post-pandemic shifts. This would avoid overbudgeting for unused capacity.
